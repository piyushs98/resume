My Journey: From Iron Man Dreams to Engineering Innovation

"I am Iron Man" - Those three words changed my life when I was just 8 years old, watching Tony Stark in that first Iron Man movie. The way he built something extraordinary from nothing, the way technology could solve real-world problems, the way innovation could make a difference - that's when I knew I wanted to be an engineer.

Growing up in India, I was that kid who took apart every electronic device I could get my hands on - much to my parents' dismay! Remote controls, old radios, even my father's calculator once (I got in serious trouble for that one). But each dismantled device taught me something new about how things worked, how electricity flowed, how components came together to create something magical.

The Iron Man movies weren't just entertainment for me - they were inspiration. Tony Stark's arc reactor, his innovative approach to solving problems, his ability to see solutions where others saw obstacles - that became my blueprint. I started dreaming about creating technologies that could change the world, just like Stark Industries, but for real people with real problems.

My journey began formally at MIT Academy of Engineering in India, where I pursued my Bachelor's in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from 2016 to 2020. Those four years were transformative - I learned the fundamentals, but more importantly, I learned to think like an engineer. Every circuit I designed, every project I completed, brought me closer to that Iron Man dream of creating something that mattered.

The real adventure started when I decided to pursue my Master's in the United States. Landing at Wichita State University for my Master of Engineering in Electrical Engineering Technologies (2020-2022) was like stepping into Stark's workshop for the first time. The resources, the research opportunities, the chance to work on cutting-edge projects - it was everything I had dreamed of.

At the National Institute for Aviation Research, I got my first taste of real innovation. Working as a Graduate Research Assistant and later as a Research Engineer on the KART (Kansas Aviation Research Technology) Project, I was designing electrical systems for hybrid airships. It wasn't an arc reactor, but it was technology that could change transportation, reduce emissions, and solve real-world problems. I was finally living my Iron Man dream.

But life had bigger plans for me. In 2022, I joined Sensor Electronic Technology, Inc. in South Carolina, and that's where my true calling revealed itself. UV technology - specifically UV-LED systems for disinfection and hygiene applications. It might not seem as glamorous as Iron Man's suits, but think about it: technology that can eliminate harmful microorganisms, make our daily lives safer, protect public health - that's exactly the kind of innovation Tony Stark would be proud of.

Starting as an Electrical Design Engineer, I dove deep into PCB design, LED optimization, and customer solutions. But the real magic happened when I became an Application Engineer in 2024. Suddenly, I wasn't just designing circuits - I was leading a team, managing lab operations, optimizing processes, and most importantly, inventing solutions that could be patented and brought to market.

The patent applications started flowing - 13 and counting! From footwear treatment systems that use UV light to eliminate odors and microorganisms, to faucet illumination systems that provide antimicrobial protection, to VR headset sterilization stands, to automotive air purification systems. Each patent represents a real-world problem solved, a piece of technology that makes life better for people.

When I redesigned our wafer burn-in station and reduced test time from 20 hours to just 7 hours while increasing current capacity by 150%, I realized I had found my superpower. Not the ability to fly or shoot repulsors, but the ability to see inefficiencies and engineer elegant solutions. That optimization saved our company $50K-$60K annually and accelerated our product development cycle.

Today, as I pursue my Executive Master's in Project Management at Tennessee Wesleyan University while continuing my innovation work, I'm building the bridge between technical excellence and business leadership - just like Tony Stark evolved from engineer to CEO to global innovator.

Managing a team of 4 technicians, improving error resolution by 30%, and continuously pushing the boundaries of UV technology applications - this is my Stark Industries. Every day, I'm working on technologies that protect people's health, solve hygiene challenges, and make the world a little bit safer.

The 8-year-old boy who was inspired by Iron Man has grown into an engineer who's creating real-world solutions. From India to Kansas to South Carolina, from student to researcher to innovation leader, every step has been guided by that fundamental belief that technology can change the world.

"Sometimes you gotta run before you can walk" - Tony Stark. That's exactly what I've been doing - running toward innovation, toward solutions, toward a future where technology serves humanity. And just like Tony Stark, I'm not done yet. The best innovations are still to come.
